Family-Friendly Rice Village: Kid-Approved Restaurants & Activities

December 20, 2025 Rice Village Shops 5 min read

Rice Village isn't just for foodies and shoppers — it's a fantastic destination for families, too. From kid-friendly restaurants with great food (for adults, too) to fun activities that keep little ones entertained, here's your family guide to the Village.

What Are the Best Kid-Friendly Restaurants in Rice Village?

Shake Shack

The gold standard of family-friendly dining. Excellent burgers, crispy fries, and milkshakes that kids (and let's be honest, adults) go crazy for. The casual counter-service format means no stress about keeping kids quiet at a fancy table.

Torchy's Tacos

Tacos are universally kid-friendly, and Torchy's makes some of the best in Houston. The queso is legendary, and the build-your-own option lets picky eaters customize. Crayons and coloring sheets keep little hands busy.

Jason's Deli

A Houston institution with a dedicated kids' menu, a free ice cream machine (yes, really), and salad bar that makes parents happy. The relaxed atmosphere is perfect for families.

Chipotle

Fast, customizable, and kid-tested. Quesadillas, rice bowls, and chips are easy wins for young diners, and the build-your-own format makes even picky eaters happy.

Insomnia Cookies

For dessert, nothing beats warm cookies fresh from the oven. Insomnia Cookies is a treat destination that kids will beg to visit again. Pro tip: the cookie ice cream sandwich is worth the mess.

What Family Activities Are There in Rice Village?

The Menil Collection

Free admission makes the Menil a budget-friendly family outing. Many kids are fascinated by the modern art, and the grassy campus is perfect for running around between gallery visits. The Menil offers family guides and occasional children's programs.

Half Price Books

Take the kids to the children's section and let them pick out a book. At half price (or less), you can afford to say yes to more than one. It's a great way to encourage reading while enjoying a cool, air-conditioned space.

Rice University Campus

The beautiful Rice University campus (see our Rice University events) is just steps from the Village. Kids love running through the oak-shaded quads, and the architecture is stunning. It's a great place for a family walk after lunch.

Window Shopping & Treasure Hunting

Older kids enjoy browsing Rice Village's unique shops. Village Thrift is a treasure hunt for vintage finds, and the toy and novelty sections at various shops keep kids engaged.

What Tips Should Families Know About Rice Village?

  • Stroller-friendly: Most Rice Village sidewalks are wide and well-maintained. Stroller navigation is generally easy.
  • Restrooms: Most restaurants and shops have accessible restrooms. Shake Shack and Jason's Deli are reliable options.
  • Timing: Visit during off-peak hours (weekday lunch or early Saturday) for a more relaxed experience with kids.
  • Parking: The garages are easier than street parking when you're wrangling car seats and strollers.

Rice Village proves that family-friendly doesn't have to mean boring. Great food, free museums, and a walkable neighborhood — what more could a family ask for?
